Color theory

Working with colors is actually a kind of witchcraft, pardon me, 'which craft' epressed using hex codes or calling names into a structure, known as using named colors.

A hex code is a hexadecimal number, consisting of a tag (#, compare with tagging), followed by 3 pairs of characters, representing the intensity of red, green and blue in the color respectively: #<rr><gg><bb>.

To illustrate: Black would be written as #000000 or #000 (shorthand) and white as #ffffff or #fff (shorthand).

Hardening and softening

HLS and HSV colors are alternative representations of the RGB color model. The HSL model attempts to resemble more perceptual color models such as the Natural Color System (NCS) or Munsell color system.

The HLS colors approach is often used to create pastel colors (gentle colors with high value but lower saturation - usually around 50% - than 'hard' colors). Compare color saturation with efforts during communicating, to 'saturate' a message into others. Too little or too much could generate planned or unexpected 'special effects'.

Originals and derivates

  • Primary colors are like primary specialisms for specific purposes.

  • Secondary colors are 2 mixed primary colors.

  • Tertiary colors are derived from one primary and one secondary color.

  • Inverse colors come from the diametrically opposite position in the color wheel. See opponent color. In CSS:
